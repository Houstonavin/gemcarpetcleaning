Commercial cleaning frequency guide for Lacey facilities

The right cleaning frequency balances appearance, asset life, and operations. Over-cleaning wastes budget; under-cleaning creates compounding restoration costs.

High-traffic office carpets

Entry zones and circulation paths usually need more frequent extraction than private offices. A mixed plan (spot + scheduled deep clean) is usually most efficient.

Tile and hard floors

Retail and healthcare-adjacent environments often require tighter floor intervals due to visible soil and slip risk. Include periodic deep scrub intervals, not only daily mopping.

Kitchen and degreasing zones

For commercial food operations, frequency should align with use intensity and compliance checks. Documented recurring service supports cleaner inspections and safer work areas.
