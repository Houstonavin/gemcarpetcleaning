Move out carpet cleaning: checklist for tenants & landlords

Carpet is often on the move out punch list. Booking too late leaves wet fiber on walk through day; booking too early can mean new scuffs before keys are turned in; timing matters.

Explore our services and pricing for Lacey, WA, or book a cleaning when you are ready.

Leases vary: some require “professionally cleaned” carpets; others specify receipt or before and after documentation. Clarify expectations with your landlord or property manager before you schedule so you order the right scope, carpet only versus whole home packages, and whether odor treatment is needed.

Before we arrive

Remove small items and breakables from rooms; move light furniture if your agreement places furniture responsibility on you. Heavy items may stay, tell us in advance so we plan around them. Vacuuming loose debris helps us focus on embedded soil during the clean.

Odor and pets

Pet odor treatment is often a separate line item from standard extraction. If spots return after surface cleaning, enzyme or flush-level work may apply, share pet history when booking so we quote accurately and leave enough dry time before inspection.

Landlords

Between tenants, a documented clean supports faster re-listing and clearer security-deposit conversations. We can scope carpet-only (up to three bedrooms in our published tiers) or full home options depending on unit size and condition.

Final walk through

Allow carpets to dry fully before final photos; fans and HVAC on “fan” mode help in Lacey’s climate. Keep a copy of your booking confirmation and any add ons for your records.

FAQ

Should I schedule carpet cleaning before or after my move out walk through?

Schedule early enough that carpets can dry fully before photos or inspection. Wet fiber can look uneven and may not reflect final appearance.

Does standard carpet cleaning always include pet odor treatment?

Not always. Severe or repeated pet accidents may need enzyme or flush level work quoted separately from standard extraction.

What documentation should I keep for my landlord or property manager?

Keep your booking confirmation, scope of work, and any add ons. If your lease requires professional cleaning, match the wording to what you ordered.
